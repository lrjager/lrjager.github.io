Leah Jager

About

Leah Jager is an Assistant Scientist in the Department of Biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. Her primary interest is in statistics and data science education; she teaches biostatistics and data analysis to both undergraduates and graduate students at Johns Hopkins. Her research focuses on human/data interaction, which involves studying the way people do data analysis. She also holds an appointment in the Johns Hopkins Biostatistics Center, where she provided statistical support to researchers across the Johns Hopkins community through walk-in clinics, short-term consultations, and longer-term grant participation.
